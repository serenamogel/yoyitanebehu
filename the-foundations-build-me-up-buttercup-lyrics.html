About

The Foundations were a British soul band formed in 1967. The group consisted of seven members, including lead vocalist Clem Curtis and guitarist Alan Warner.

Their sound was heavily influenced by Motown and other American soul music, and they had several hit singles in the late 1960s, including “Baby Now That I’ve Found You” and “Build Me Up Buttercup”.

The group disbanded in the early 1970s, but their music has continued to be popular and influential.

FAQs

What happened to the Foundations pop group?

The Foundations were a British soul/R&B group that formed in London in 1967. They had a string of hits in the late 1960s and early 1970s, including “Build Me Up Buttercup,” “Baby Now That I’ve Found You,” and “Back on My Feet Again.” The group disbanded in 1970, but they have reunited several times since then.

Who was the lead singer for the Foundations?

The lead singer for the Foundations was Clem Curtis. He was born in Trinidad in 1949 and moved to England with his family when he was a child. Curtis was a charismatic performer with a powerful voice. He left the Foundations in 1970, but he continued to record and perform solo. He died in 2017 at the age of 67.

What type of band is the Foundations?

The Foundations were a soul/R&B group. They were influenced by a variety of musical styles, including ska, rock and roll, and Motown. Their music was characterized by Curtis’s powerful vocals, the group’s tight harmonies, and their infectious melodies.

What is the foundation of all music?

The foundation of all music is rhythm. Rhythm is the underlying beat that gives music its structure and groove. It is what makes us want to tap our feet and dance. Without rhythm, music would be nothing more than a collection of sounds.
