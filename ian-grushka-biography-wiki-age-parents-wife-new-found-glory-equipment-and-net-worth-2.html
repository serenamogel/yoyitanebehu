Who is Ian Grushka? Ian Grushka Biography and Wiki

Ian Grushka born on September 4, 1977, as Ian Ryan Grushka is an American musician and songwriter. He is well known as one of the founding members and the bassist of Florida rock band New Found Glory. Grushka was also the bassist in the band’s now-defunct side-project, the International Superheroes of Hardcore, where he performed under the pseudonym of “Sgt. Soy”.

Ian Grushka New Found Glory

In 1997, Grushka played bass with Jordan Pundik (vocals) in the band “Inner City Men,” and later “Flip 60.” They hired Stephen Klein (guitar), who met Pundik at Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School and had previously played with him in the band “Fallview” after disbanding “Flip 60.” The threesome started to jam together. Later, they asked Joe “Taco Joe” Marino to play drums while training in Grushka ‘s garage. Soon afterward, former Shai Hulud vocalist Chad Gilbert (lead guitar) joined in to complete the quintet. The band would become New Found Glory.

Grushka has collaborated with a variety of musicians to his work with New Found Glory. He plays bass in the track “Cobwebs” on Make It Bleed, DBY ‘s 2004 release. He also plays bass on the track “Boy Without Batteries” on the 2013 album Heart Attack (Man Overboard album) on Man Overboard (band).
