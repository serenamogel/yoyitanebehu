Donte Greene Has Career Earnings Over $5 Million

According to estimations by HoopsHype, Donte Greene has amassed career earnings of over $5 million.

The exact value of his earnings is $5,153,283. He made it throughout his NBA career, playing for different teams throughout five seasons. If the current inflation rate is considered, Greene’s total earnings will reach close to seven million dollars.

The 34-year-old American basketball player had a world at his feet when he first came to NBA in 2008 after the Memphis Grizzlies picked him in the 28th overall draft. But he was treated to the Houston Rockets soon after he arrived at the club.

The Rockets eventually parted ways with him and traded him to the Sacramento Kings in August 2008. Greene and the Kings agreed to a contract that would result in him earning $971,160 yearly.

The Sacramento Kings, who were pleased with his play and opted to raise his salary after the 2009–10 season, did so as part of a contract extension arrangement that included an increase in his annual compensation to $1,044,000.

And for the following two seasons, Greene saw a rise in his annual earnings. For the 2010-11 season, he made $1,116,840, and for the 2011-12 season, he earned the most in a year in his career as he racked up earnings of $2 million that season, making it the highest earning year of his career.

Greene inked a contract with the Atléticos de San Germán of Puerto Rico on March 11, 2013, to play with them during the 2013 BSN season. After playing just one game for Atléticos, he decided to part ways with the club on April 5, 2013.

He then agreed to a contract with the Memphis Grizzlies on April 17, 2013, worth $5,387, to play for the team for the rest of the season. In July 2013, he became the Grizzlies’ roster to participate in the 2013 NBA Summer League.

Donte Greene Career In A Nutshell

Donte Greene had a stellar career after entering the NBA in 2008 after being selected by the Memphis Grizzlies.

The Grizzlies picked with the 28th overall choice in that year’s draft. Before that, Donte had a successful basketball career at the university level with the Syracuse Orange of the NCAA.

Greene was selected for inclusion in the U.S. Under-18 Men’s National Team. A year later, he was picked to play on the Nike Brand All-America Team and participated in the Jordan Classic. Similarly, he was honored with the state of Maryland’s Gatorade Player of the Year award.

The following year, Greene was nonetheless picked by Memphis at the 28th overall spot in the 2008 NBA Draft. After that, he went on to play with both the Houston Rockets and the Sacramento Kings.

Despite his promise, Greene still played professionally in Puerto Rico in 2013. He never reached his full potential. Since then, his professional basketball career has brought him across the world, with stops in Germany, China, Saudi Arabia, Lebanon, the Dominican Republic, Taiwan, and, most recently, the Big 3 league.

Following his departure from the NBA, Greene went on to play professionally in different parts of the world. As a result, his popularity among NBA fans began to wane over time. In the 2017–18 season, he participated in his last professional basketball game with Champville SC.
