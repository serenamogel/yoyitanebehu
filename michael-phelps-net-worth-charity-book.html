Michael Phelps is a famous American former competitive swimmer and athlete with a net worth of $65 Million.

Michael Fred Phelps II was born on 30th June of 1985, the youngest of three children.

Moreover, his family lived just outside of Baltimore, Maryland, where all three children learned to swim early.

The most successful and most decorated olympian holds 28 Olympic medals, including 23 gold medals.

He’s considered one of the greatest swimmers of all time. This former competitive swimmer is a world record holder in the men’s 400-meter individual medley.

Moreover, he’s also the former long course world record holder in the 100-meter butterfly, 200-meter freestyle, 200-meter butterfly, and 200-meter individual medley.

Michael Phelps: Book Publications

In addition to his excellent career, the swimmer with 28 medals has also written books.

Till now, he has written two books named Beneath the Surface: My Story (2008) and No Limits: The Will to Succeed (2009).

In his book, Beneath the Surface: My Story, he tells the story of his life, not a swimming diary.

In addition, he talks about his arrest in 2004 and his thoughts on the 2008 Olympics.

Despite Phelps’s winnings in swimming, his story is a tale of overcoming obstacles and redemption.

In his other book, No Limits: The Will to Succeed, Phelps talks about his family, his coach, and his passion for the sport, as well as the lessons he has learned from overcoming obstacles.

From Michael’s early days as a teenager to his achievements as a world champion, this book will reveal the inspiring story of Michael Phelps.

Moreover, he shares his inspiring anecdotes, as well as valuable lessons learned from his experiences.

Michael Phelps: Career

Michael began swimming at the age of seven, and people quickly recognized his talent. He was a part of the U.S summer Olympic team in 2000.

In 2001, Phelps became the youngest male swimmer to set a world record in the 200-meter butterfly.

At the 2001 world championships, he broke his own record and won his first international medal just after graduating high school.

Phelps also holds the record for most Olympic gold medal wins. In 2008  he won 8 gold medals in the Beijing games.

Similarly, at the 2012 summer Olympics in London, Phelps won four gold and two silver medals.

Moreover, he announced his retirement on August 12th, 2016. To date, he has set the record for the most medal wins by an Olympic athlete.
