Legal Issue & Controversy

Tory has been in several legal troubles and controversies in his career.

He issued a challenge to Drake in 2010, saying if the OVO leader heard Tory’s music and didn’t like it, the youngin would pay his elder $10,000. Drake never acknowledged the challenge, though.

Similarly, he had beef with rapper Joyner Lucas, Jacquees, and Don Q, which resulted in them dissing each other.

On March 26, 2016, as a performance was taking place in Midland, Texas, security personnel and people in the crowd got into violent altercations.

Lanez continued by telling the audience to “f*** shit up,” which sparked a riot and required cops to call off the performance.

There were several arrests, and the concert venue and organizers considered suing him.

Moreover, when the police were called to a house party that went violent in July 2020, they discovered a concealed firearm in Tory’s car and detained him.

Megan Thee Stallion, a fellow rapper, was in the vehicle with her foot hurt.

Megan later disclosed that a gunshot inflicted the wound and that Tory was the shooter, despite the initial reports claiming that broken glass was to blame.

In October 2020, Lanez was charged with three felonies, including an attack with a semiautomatic weapon.

Moreover, he was assigned a restraining order preventing him from speaking to Megan or approaching her closer than 100 feet.

Since Tory broke the restraining order in August 2021, his bail was raised from $190,000 to $250,000.

Moreover, he was detained in April 2022 for breaking a restraining order related to the case, but he was soon released after posting a $350,000 bond.

Tory Lanez | Charity

During the Coronavirus outbreak, Tory assisted families struggling to meet their urgent needs by using the show’s viral success to their advantage.

He started the “Tory Lanez Dream City Fund” in 2020 to support Covid-19 relief operations.

“The Dream City Project,” which he and his father established in 2012 to encourage and assist young people, and the “Dream Center,” a Los Angeles-based nonprofit, joined forces to create funds.

Together, they gathered money to help underprivileged families in Los Angeles and other areas across the country by providing food and diapers.

Tory expressed his support for Haitian immigrants in 2021.

He personally flew to Del Rio, Texas, close to the scene of the crisis, and delivered supplies for immigrants still being held in detention centers.

Towels, blankets, and other goods were distributed with Lanez’s assistance.

Tory Lanez | Career

Tory released the mixtape “T.L 2 T.O.” in 2009.

After seeing a freestyling video, rapper Sean Kingston requested a meeting with him at the beginning of 2010.

After that, Lanez signed a record deal with Kingston’s “Time is Money Entertainment” in 2011.

He went on to release four mixtapes that year, including “Just Landed,” “Playing for Keeps,” “One Verse One Hearse,” and “Mr. 1 Verse Killah.”

He later released several other mixtapes, including “Lost Cause” (2014), “Conflicts of My Soul: The 416 Story” (2013), “Sincerely Tory” (2012), and “Chixtape” (2011).

“I Told You,” Lanez’s first studio album, was released under Interscope Records/Mad Love Records on August 19, 2016.

The famous singles “Say It” and “Luv” was included on the Gold album.

His first album peaked at number one on the Billboard Top Rap Albums chart and number four on the Billboard 200 chart.
