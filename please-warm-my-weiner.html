Overall Meaning

The song "Please Warm My Weiner" by Bo Carter, recorded in 1935, is widely interpreted as a sexual innuendo. The lyrics convey the message of a man asking a woman to warm up his "wiener" or penis, as it does not feel right when it's cold. The song uses indirect language to convey a message of sexual desire, and it's unsurprising that it was perceived as a 'blue' or obscene song at the time of its release.


The song can be seen as a representation of society's traditional gender roles, where men are expected to initiate sex and women are expected to submit. The lyrics depict a man asking for sexual pleasure, and the woman is expected to comply with the request. It's interesting to note that the song was recorded during the Depression era, when the economic turmoil forced many people to explore unconventional ways of making a living.


Bo Carter was known for his humorous and sexually charged songs, and "Please Warm My Weiner" was one of his most popular songs. Although it was banned from the radio and considered too controversial at the time, it remained popular among blues enthusiasts and musicians.
