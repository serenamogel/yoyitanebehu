Paul Theron Silas, also known as Paul Silas, was a highly esteemed American professional basketball player and NBA head coach. His exceptional rebounding capabilities and defensive prowess were instrumental in the success of three NBA championship teams.

During his illustrious 16-season career, Silas notably played for the Celtics, and he had the honor of being LeBron James’s first coach in the NBA. As a player, he secured two championships with Boston and another with Seattle during the 1970s. Recognized for his accomplishments, Silas was inducted into the College Basketball Hall of Fame in 2017. Sadly, Paul Silas passed away on Sunday, December 11, 2022.
